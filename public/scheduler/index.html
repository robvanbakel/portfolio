About this project

At a former employer, our work schedule was created per week. Every employee got their weekly schedule sent by them via a Slack message by the Planning department. This system worked, but was prone to user error as shift time scould vary greatly from day to day without any option to automatically import your work schedule into your phone's calendar. To simplify the lives of my colleagues and myself, I set out to solve this problem.

The goal

The goal was simple: Employers would have be able to easily and quickly put their work schedule from Slack into their own calendars, without having to manually type anything. The less user interaction, the better.

Because I couln't access the Slack account that was used to send out the weekly schedules, I would have to tackle this project from the outside. With just a few lines of plain text in my messages on Slack as my only input, I got to work.

Version 1

My first idea was to let users copy the message's body text and paste it onto a web page, from where the text would be undergoing some RegEx-magic™ and be parsed into an 

.ics
file, which the employee could then download and import in their agenda.

This worked, but completely missed the goal. Every aspect about this workflow was far from quick or hassle-free. I counted them, it took 16 clicks. Besides that, every solution that included the terms '.ics file' and 'download and import' were out of the question. Time for version 2.

Slack Bot

To enable users to put their schedules in their calendars as quickly as possible, this had to be done without leaving Slack. I got to work with the Slack API and the Scheduler Bot was born.

From within Slack, employees can activate the bot, which will then directly read the message, runs the parsing logic, generates the calendar items and outputs a preview to the user. With one tap, the user can then add all shifts to their calendar, including the shift's name, location and of course, start and end times.

Final score: 3 clicks.
